Incarceration Rate By State

Today, the United States stands as the country with the second highest incarceration rate in the world. Surpassed only by the Republic of Seychelles, a tiny country with less than 100,000 citizens plagued by Somali Pirates. With an incarceration rate vastly surpassing any other major country, the United States is plagued by a penal system that seems to be used more like a weapon than a method of rehabilitation. Raw census data from http://www.bjs.gov/index.cfm?ty=pbdetail&iid=5177

Incarceration Rate By State

With a significant proportion of inmates behind bars due to non-violent offenses such as drug possession, a need for prison reform is clear and present. In the aftermath of the "tough on crime" ideology of the 1980s, a staggering prison population imposes economic, political, and social costs. Aside from the impact of the War on Drugs and the ensuing wave of incarceration, another source of the skyrocketing incarcerated population is from prosecutors. Charged with the power to pressure defendants into plea bargains with the threat of long sentences, prosecutors are incentivized to be brutally harsh on criminals in order to look tough for election time.
